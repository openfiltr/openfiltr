## Design Context

### Users
Adults playing a premium casual block puzzle, mainly on mobile but also on desktop and tablet. They want instant access to gameplay during repeat short sessions, with no friction, clutter, or hoops before they can start playing. Some players will also explore optional coaching, themes, colours, and gameplay analytics after the core play loop hooks them.

### Brand Personality
Minimalist, refined, focused. The interface should feel calm and polished like a quality sudoku app, while still being engaging enough to encourage repeat play. It should support concentration, reduce frustration, and make the game feel premium rather than noisy or gimmicky.

### Aesthetic Direction
Clean, uncluttered, touch-friendly design with a premium puzzle-game sensibility. Visual inspiration should lean closer to elegant sudoku products than to flashy arcade games. The main play surface should dominate, with secondary controls and deeper features present but visually recessed until needed. Avoid generic mobile utility styling, cluttered game chrome, and childish presentation.

### Design Principles
1. Gameplay first: the board and piece placement should always dominate the interface.
2. Calm clarity: remove visual noise and keep the hierarchy obvious at a glance.
3. Premium tactility: interactions should feel deliberate, smooth, and satisfying without becoming flashy.
4. Optional depth: coaching, themes, and analytics should be easy to access without competing with core play.
5. Cross-device composure: maintain touch-friendly usability on mobile while scaling gracefully to tablet and desktop.
